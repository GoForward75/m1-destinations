Discover Korea's East Sea where most people visit and witness the first sunrise of the year. Immerse yourself in Korea's mouthwatering cuisine and fascinating culture during the day and party the night away at Seoul's bustling and extravagant nightlife

SPAIN

In Spain, the New Year celebrations start as early as December 31. The streets are filled with people and music, but if you're looking for a huge party, visit the famous Puerta del Sol Square in Madrid where it turns into a large festivity once the clock strikes twelve.

UNITED KINGDOM

During New Year's Eve, pubs and restaurants all over the UK gear up for parties that last until dawn. The next afternoon, more than 10,000 performers from all over the world will gather for a massive New Year's Day parade in London.

Gather with more than one million people over Sydney Harbour and watch the spectacular annual fireworks display. It's so extraordinary that is also anticipated by people from all over the world as well.

In the Philippines, Filipinos go all out during New Year's Eve celebrations. From the endless array of food, firecrackers that come in various shapres and sizes, to interesting traditions, welcoming 2018 in the Pearl of the Orient Seas is definitely a must-try.
